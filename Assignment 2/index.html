What is an Asylum Seeker?

Analysing Asylum Decisions Given To Applicants

Here we can see how many of each decision was given to an applicant current residing in a country. The following bar chart shows the top 10 countries per each decision type. Now, we can visualise how many of a country's yearly applications are actually recognised or rejected etc. There are four decisions that can be given to an asylum application; these are recognised (application is granted), complementary protection (application is granted for those who cannot return to their home country), rejected decisions (application is rejected) and otherwise closed (application is closed for administrative reasons). The number of total decisions can also be displayed with the most applications coming out of Iraq in 2020.





This line chart shows how many applicants there are each year that apply to leave a country. It has a select box to filter a specific country. It shows the yearly number of applications coming from a country, and Iraq has the most applications in 2015 with 215,382 applications.




This line chart shows how many applicants there are each year that apply to enter a country. For Australia, it had a peak application number in 2017 with 49,868 asylum applications. Australia is a popular refuge country as they are known for their multiculturalism and embrace people of different races.
